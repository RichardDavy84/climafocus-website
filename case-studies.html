Case Studies

Real-World Impact Through Data and Expertise

Learn how Climate Compass has helped organizations worldwide navigate climate challenges using tailored data, robust analysis, and hands-on collaboration.

Northern Norway Parks

Challenge: Understanding and mitigating the impacts of climate change on sensitive park ecosystems, including permafrost thaw and altered river flows.

Approach: We held in-depth consultations with park rangers, provided high-resolution climate data for different scenarios out to 2100, and recommended sector-specific metrics for ongoing monitoring.

Outcome: Improved river management strategies, reduced erosion risks, and a clear adaptation roadmap for the next decade.

Memorandum for environmental advocacy group

Challenge: Deliver a concise, policy-ready memo on climate change implications for the Central Arctic Ocean.

Approach: We synthesized the latest data on Arctic warming, sea ice retreat, and ecosystem vulnerabilities, then produced an authoritative briefing for senior management within the advocacy group.

Outcome: Enhanced advocacy efforts, stronger messaging in policy debates, and greater public awareness.

Wind Park in Scotland

Challenge: Assessing the wind power density at 30 meter resolution for a Scottish hillside. Assess the effect of including two “blackout” periods when turbines were turned off for a few weeks each year for maintenance.

Approach: We downscaled wind power density to 30 meter resolution using hourly wind speeds, built a virtual wind generator to simulate power output in an interactive map, and created an interactive tool to assess the impact of downtime intervals on annual yield, while also exploring how future climate scenarios might shift wind patterns.

Outcome: Clear recommendations on scheduling maintenance and optimizing energy production, plus a forward-looking plan for adaptation to projected wind variability.
