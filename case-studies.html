Case Studies

Real-World Impact Through Data and Expertise

Learn how Climate Compass has helped organizations worldwide navigate climate challenges using tailored data, robust analysis, and hands-on collaboration.

Northern Norway Parks

Challenge: Understanding and mitigating the impacts of climate change on sensitive park ecosystems and ecosystem services, including river flood risk and dangerous snow conditions.

Approach: We held in-depth consultations with park rangers, provided high-resolution climate metrics applicable to their priorities for different scenarios out to 2100.

Outcome: Updated river management strategies, heightened focus on erosion risks, and a clear adaptation roadmap for the next decade.

Memorandum for Environmental Advocacy Group

Challenge: Deliver a concise, policy-ready memo on climate change implications for the Central Arctic Ocean.

Approach: We synthesized the latest data on Arctic warming, sea ice retreat, and ecosystem vulnerabilities, then produced an authoritative briefing for senior management within the advocacy group.

Outcome: Enhanced advocacy efforts, stronger messaging in policy debates, and greater public awareness.

Wind Park in Scotland

Challenge: Assessing the wind power density at 30 meter resolution for a Scottish hillside, factoring in two “blackout” periods for annual maintenance downtime.

Approach: We downscaled wind power density to 30 m resolution using hourly wind data, built a virtual wind generator to simulate power output, and developed an interactive tool to assess the impact of downtime intervals on annual yield—while considering how future scenarios might shift wind patterns.

Outcome: Clear recommendations on scheduling maintenance and optimizing energy production, plus a forward-looking plan for adaptation to projected wind variability.
